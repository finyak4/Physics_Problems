Two-Slit Interference

Young's experiment demonstrates the wave nature of light. Two slits act as coherent point sources, creating an interference pattern.

Wave Parameters

Wave Equations

1. Superposition

The total displacement is the sum of spherical waves from sources r₁ and r₂:

u(r,t) = A / |r - r₁| · sin(k|r - r₁| - ωt) +
A / |r - r₂| · sin(k|r - r₂| - ωt)

2. Conditions for Maxima

Constructive interference (bright fringes) occurs when the path difference Δr is an integer multiple of the wavelength:

Δr = |r - r₁| - |r - r₂| ≈ d·sin(θ)
d·sin(θ) = nλ   (n = 0, ±1, ±2...)

3. Time-Averaged Intensity

The intensity plotted on the right screen is proportional to the square of the amplitude:

I ∝ umax² = I₁ + I₂ + 2√(I₁I₂)·cos(kΔr)
