Analytical Solutions

a) Equation of the Trajectory

To find the geometric shape, eliminate time t from x and y:

x = a·cos(ωt)   ⇒   x/a = cos(ωt)
y = b·sin(ωt)   ⇒   y/b = sin(ωt)

Squaring and adding them together yields:

(x/a)² + (y/b)² = 1

This means the trajectory lies entirely on the surface of an elliptical cylinder extending along the Z-axis. Since z = bt, the point moves upwards at a constant rate while tracing the ellipse, forming an elliptical helix.

b) Path Length

The path length S is the integral of the speed v(t):

vx = -aω·sin(ωt)
vy = bω·cos(ωt)
vz = b

Speed v = √(vx² + vy² + vz²) :

S = ∫ √(a²ω²sin²(ωt) + b²ω²cos²(ωt) + b²) dt

Generally, this is an elliptic integral of the second kind. However, in the special case where a = b (circular helix):

S = ∫ √(a²ω²(sin²(ωt) + cos²(ωt)) + a²) dt
S = ∫ a√(ω² + 1) dt
S = at₀√(ω² + 1)
