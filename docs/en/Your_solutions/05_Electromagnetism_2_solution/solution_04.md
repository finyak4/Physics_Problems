# Solution 4: Magnetic Torque

## Problem Statement
A rectangular loop of wire with dimensions $10 \text{ cm}$ by $5 \text{ cm}$ carries a current of $2 \text{ A}$. A uniform magnetic field of $B = 0.3 \text{ T}$ is applied parallel to the plane of the loop. What is the magnitude of the magnetic torque on the loop?

## Step-by-Step Solution

**1. Recall the formula for Magnetic Torque:**

The magnitude of the magnetic torque $\tau$ on a current-carrying loop is given by:
$$ \tau = m B \sin\theta $$
where:

* $m = I A$ is the magnetic dipole moment of the loop ($I$ is current, $A$ is area).
* $B$ is the magnitude of the uniform magnetic field.
* $\theta$ is the angle between the area vector $\mathbf{A}$ (which is perpendicular to the plane of the loop) and the magnetic field vector $\mathbf{B}$.

**2. Identify the given values:**

* Current, $I = 2 \text{ A}$
* Length, $l = 10 \text{ cm} = 0.1 \text{ m}$
* Width, $w = 5 \text{ cm} = 0.05 \text{ m}$
* Magnetic field, $B = 0.3 \text{ T}$
* Since the magnetic field is applied *parallel* to the plane of the loop, the angle between the magnetic field and the area vector (which is perpendicular to the plane) is $\theta = 90^\circ$. Thus, $\sin(90^\circ) = 1$.

**3. Calculate the Area and Magnetic Moment:**

* Area of the rectangular loop, $A = l \times w$:
$$ A = 0.1 \text{ m} \times 0.05 \text{ m} = 0.005 \text{ m}^2 $$
* Magnetic dipole moment, $m = I \times A$:
$$ m = 2 \text{ A} \times 0.005 \text{ m}^2 = 0.01 \text{ A}\cdot\text{m}^2 $$

**4. Calculate the Magnetic Torque:**
Substitute the values into the torque formula:

$$ \tau = (0.01 \text{ A}\cdot\text{m}^2) \times (0.3 \text{ T}) \times \sin(90^\circ) $$

$$ \tau = 0.01 \times 0.3 \times 1 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m} $$

$$ \tau = 0.003 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m} $$

**Final Answer:**

The magnitude of the magnetic torque on the loop is **$0.003 \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}$** (or $3 \times 10^{-3} \text{ N}\cdot\text{m}$).
