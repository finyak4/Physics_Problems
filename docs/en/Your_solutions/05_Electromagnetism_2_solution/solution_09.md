# Solution 9: Refraction (Snell's Law)

## Problem Statement
A light ray travels from air ($n=1.00$) into glass ($n=1.50$). If the angle of incidence is $30^\circ$, what is the angle of refraction?

## Step-by-Step Solution

**1. Recall Snell's Law:**
Snell's law describes the relationship between the angles of incidence and refraction when light passes between two different isotropic media:
$$ n_1 \sin\theta_1 = n_2 \sin\theta_2 $$
where:
* $n_1$ is the refractive index of the first medium (air).
* $\theta_1$ is the angle of incidence.
* $n_2$ is the refractive index of the second medium (glass).
* $\theta_2$ is the angle of refraction.

**2. Identify the given values:**
* Index of refraction of air, $n_1 = 1.00$
* Angle of incidence, $\theta_1 = 30^\circ$
* Index of refraction of glass, $n_2 = 1.50$

**3. Calculate the angle of refraction $\theta_2$:**
Substitute the known values into Snell's law:
$$ 1.00 \times \sin(30^\circ) = 1.50 \times \sin\theta_2 $$
We know that $\sin(30^\circ) = 0.5$.
$$ 0.5 = 1.50 \times \sin\theta_2 $$
Solve for $\sin\theta_2$:
$$ \sin\theta_2 = \frac{0.5}{1.50} = \frac{1}{3} \approx 0.3333 $$
Now, take the inverse sine (arcsine) to find the angle:
$$ \theta_2 = \arcsin\left(\frac{1}{3}\right) $$
$$ \theta_2 \approx 19.47^\circ $$

**Final Answer:**
The angle of refraction is approximately **$19.5^\circ$**.
