# Solution 8: EM Spectrum

## Problem Statement
List the following types of electromagnetic radiation in order of increasing wavelength: Infrared, Ultraviolet, Microwaves, X-rays, Radio waves, Gamma rays.

## Step-by-Step Solution

**1. Understand the Electromagnetic Spectrum:**
The electromagnetic spectrum categorizes different types of EM radiation based on their wavelengths (or frequencies).
* **Gamma rays** have the highest energy, highest frequency, and the **shortest wavelength**.
* **Radio waves** have the lowest energy, lowest frequency, and the **longest wavelength**.

**2. Order the categories:**
From shortest wavelength (highest energy) to longest wavelength (lowest energy), the standard order is:
1. Gamma rays ($< 0.01 \text{ nm}$)
2. X-rays ($0.01 \text{ nm} - 10 \text{ nm}$)
3. Ultraviolet (UV) ($10 \text{ nm} - 400 \text{ nm}$)
4. Visible light ($400 \text{ nm} - 700 \text{ nm}$) (Not in the list)
5. Infrared (IR) ($700 \text{ nm} - 1 \text{ mm}$)
6. Microwaves ($1 \text{ mm} - 1 \text{ m}$)
7. Radio waves ($> 1 \text{ m}$)

**3. Arrange the given list:**
Following the order established above, the list in order of **increasing wavelength** is:
1. Gamma rays
2. X-rays
3. Ultraviolet
4. Infrared
5. Microwaves
6. Radio waves

**Final Answer:**
The correct order of increasing wavelength is: **Gamma rays < X-rays < Ultraviolet < Infrared < Microwaves < Radio waves**.
