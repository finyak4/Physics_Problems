# Solution 10: Speed of Light in Media

## Problem Statement
What is the speed of light in a diamond, which has an index of refraction $n = 2.42$?

## Step-by-Step Solution

**1. Recall the relationship between speed of light and refractive index:**

The index of refraction $n$ of a material is defined as the ratio of the speed of light in a vacuum $c$ to the speed of light in that material $v$:

$$ n = \frac{c}{v} $$

**2. Identify the given values:**

* Index of refraction of diamond, $n = 2.42$
* Speed of light in vacuum, $c \approx 3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$

**3. Calculate the speed of light in diamond $v$:**

Rearrange the formula to solve for $v$:

$$ v = \frac{c}{n} $$

Substitute the given values:

$$ v = \frac{3.0 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}}{2.42} $$

$$ v \approx 1.2396 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s} $$

**Final Answer:**
The speed of light in a diamond is approximately **$1.24 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}$**.
