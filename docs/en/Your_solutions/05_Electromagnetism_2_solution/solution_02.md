# Solution 2: Ampere's Law

## Problem Statement
Two long, parallel wires are $10 \text{ cm}$ apart and carry currents of $5 \text{ A}$ in opposite directions. Calculate the magnitude and direction of the magnetic field at a point midway between the wires.

## Step-by-Step Solution

**1. Recall Ampere's Law for a Long Straight Wire:**

The magnitude of the magnetic field $B$ produced by a long, straight wire carrying a current $I$ at a distance $r$ from the wire is given by:

$$ B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r} $$

where $\mu_0 = 4\pi \times 10^{-7} \text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}$ is the vacuum permeability.

**2. Identify the given values:**

* Current in each wire, $I_1 = I_2 = 5 \text{ A}$
* Distance between the wires, $d = 10 \text{ cm} = 0.1 \text{ m}$
* Distance from each wire to the midway point, $r = \frac{d}{2} = 5 \text{ cm} = 0.05 \text{ m}$

**3. Calculate the magnetic field magnitude from one wire:**

$$ B_1 = \frac{(4\pi \times 10^{-7} \text{ T}\cdot\text{m/A}) (5 \text{ A})}{2\pi (0.05 \text{ m})} $$

$$ B_1 = \frac{2 \times 10^{-7} \times 5}{0.05} \text{ T} $$

$$ B_1 = \frac{10 \times 10^{-7}}{0.05} \text{ T} = 2 \times 10^{-5} \text{ T} = 20 \text{ μT} $$

Since the second wire carries the same current and is at the same distance, $B_2 = 20 \text{ μT}$.

**4. Determine the direction and net magnetic field:**

* Using the right-hand rule, point your thumb in the direction of the current for the first wire. Your fingers curl around the wire in the direction of the magnetic field.
* Since the currents are in opposite directions, the magnetic field lines from both wires will point in the **same direction** at the midway point between them.
* Therefore, the net magnetic field $B_{\text{net}}$ is the sum of the magnitudes of the individual fields:

$$ B_{\text{net}} = B_1 + B_2 $$

$$ B_{\text{net}} = 20 \text{ μT} + 20 \text{ μT} = 40 \text{ μT} $$

**Final Answer:**

The magnitude of the magnetic field at the midway point is **$40 \text{ μT}$** ($4 \times 10^{-5} \text{ T}$). The direction is **perpendicular to the plane containing the two wires**.
