# Solution 1: Gauss's Law

## Problem Statement
A point charge of $+2 \text{ C}$ is located at the origin. Calculate the electric flux through a spherical surface of radius $1 \text{ m}$ centered at the origin.

## Step-by-Step Solution

The standard definition of flux is the surface integral:
$$ \Phi_E = \oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{A} $$

Gauss proved that the total flux depends entirely on the charge enclosed inside the volume, regardless of the size or shape of the boundary. The 1-meter radius given in the prompt is actually irrelevant.

**1. Recall Gauss's Law:**
Gauss's Law states that the net electric flux $\Phi_E$ through any closed surface is equal to the net charge $Q_{\text{enc}}$ enclosed by that surface divided by the vacuum permittivity $\epsilon_0$:
$$ \Phi_E = \frac{Q_{\text{enc}}}{\epsilon_0} $$

**2. Identify the given values:**

* Enclosed charge $Q_{\text{enc}} = +2 \text{ C}$
* Vacuum permittivity $\epsilon_0 \approx 8.854 \times 10^{-12} \text{ C}^2 / (\text{N} \cdot \text{m}^2)$

**3. Calculate the electric flux:**

Substitute the values into the Gauss's Law equation:

$$ \Phi_E = \frac{2}{8.854 \times 10^{-12}} $$

$$ \Phi_E \approx 2.259 \times 10^{11} \text{ N} \cdot \text{m}^2 / \text{C} $$

**Final Answer:**

The total electric flux through the spherical surface is approximately **$2.26 \times 10^{11} \text{ N} \cdot \text{m}^2 / \text{C}$**.
