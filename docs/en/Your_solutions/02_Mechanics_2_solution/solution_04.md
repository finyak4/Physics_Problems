# 4. Energy & Momentum

## Problem Statement
A 0.5 kg block slides down a frictionless track from a height of 3.0 m. At the bottom, it collides and sticks to a 1.5 kg block, which is initially at rest. What is the speed of the combined mass just after the collision?

## Step-by-Step Solution

**Part 1: Velocity of the first block before collision**

1. **Apply conservation of energy:**
   To find the speed of the 0.5 kg block ($m_1$) at the bottom of the track, equate its initial potential energy to its final kinetic energy.
   $$ m_1gh = \frac{1}{2}m_1v_1^2 $$

2. **Solve for $v_1$:**
   $$ v_1 = \sqrt{2gh} $$

3. **Substitute the values:**
   With $h = 3.0\text{ m}$ and $g = 9.81\text{ m/s}^2$:
   $$ v_1 = \sqrt{2 \times 9.81 \times 3.0} = \sqrt{58.86} \approx 7.67\text{ m/s} $$

**Part 2: Speed after collision**

1. **Apply conservation of momentum:**
   The collision is perfectly inelastic, so the blocks stick together.
   $$ p_{\text{initial}} = p_{\text{final}} $$

2. **Set up the momentum equation:**
   The initial momentum is just the momentum of the first block, and the final momentum is the combined mass moving at velocity $v_f$:
   $$ m_1 v_1 = (m_1 + m_2) v_f $$

3. **Solve for $v_f$:**
   $$ v_f = \frac{m_1 v_1}{m_1 + m_2} $$

4. **Substitute the known values:**
   $$ v_f = \frac{0.5 \times 7.67}{0.5 + 1.5} = \frac{3.835}{2.0} \approx 1.92\text{ m/s} $$
