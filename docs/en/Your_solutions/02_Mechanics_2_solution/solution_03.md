# 3. Conservation of Energy

## Problem Statement
A pendulum with a length of 1.0 meter is released from an initial angle of $15^\circ$. What is the speed of the pendulum bob at the bottom of its swing?

## Step-by-Step Solution

1. **Understand the geometry of the system:**
   A pendulum of length $L = 1.0\text{ m}$ is displaced by $\theta = 15^\circ$. Let the lowest point be the reference level for potential energy ($h=0$).
2. **Calculate the initial height $h$:**
   The height $h$ from the reference level is:
   $$ h = L - L\cos\theta = L(1 - \cos\theta) $$
   Substituting the values ($\cos 15^\circ \approx 0.9659$):
   $$ h = 1.0 \times (1 - \cos 15^\circ) \approx 1.0 \times (1 - 0.9659) = 0.0341\text{ m} $$
3. **Apply conservation of mechanical energy:**
   The initial potential energy at the highest point equals the kinetic energy at the lowest point:
   $$ E_i = E_f \implies mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 $$
4. **Solve for velocity $v$:**
   The mass $m$ cancels out:
   $$ gh = \frac{1}{2}v^2 \implies v = \sqrt{2gh} $$
5. **Substitute the values:**
   Assuming $g = 9.81\text{ m/s}^2$:
   $$ v = \sqrt{2 \times 9.81 \times 0.0341} = \sqrt{0.669} \approx 0.818\text{ m/s} $$
