# 2. Harmonic Motion

## Problem Statement
A 10 kg mass is attached to a spring and oscillates according to the equation $x(t) = 0.2 \cos(10\pi t)$ (in meters). What is the spring constant $k$? What is the total mechanical energy of the system?

## Step-by-Step Solution

**Part 1: Spring Constant $k$**

1. The equation of motion is given by:
   $$ x(t) = 0.2 \cos(10\pi t) $$
   This is in the standard form $x(t) = A \cos(\omega t + \phi)$.

2. By comparing the equations, we identify the angular frequency $\omega = 10\pi \text{ rad/s}$ and the amplitude $A = 0.2 \text{ m}$.

3. The relationship between angular frequency, mass, and spring constant is:
   $$ \omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} \implies k = m\omega^2 $$

4. Substitute $m = 10 \text{ kg}$ and $\omega = 10\pi$:
   $$ k = 10 \times (10\pi)^2 = 10 \times 100\pi^2 = 1000\pi^2 \, \text{N/m} \approx 9869.6 \, \text{N/m} $$

**Part 2: Total Mechanical Energy**

1. The total mechanical energy $E$ of a simple harmonic oscillator is given by the maximum potential energy (or maximum kinetic energy):
   $$ E = \frac{1}{2} k A^2 $$

2. Substitute the values of $k$ and $A$:
   $$ E = \frac{1}{2} (1000\pi^2) (0.2)^2 = 500\pi^2 \times 0.04 = 20\pi^2 \, \text{J} \approx 197.4 \, \text{J} $$
