# 5. Inelastic Collision

## Problem Statement
A 70 kg runner moving at $3 \text{ m/s}$ jumps onto a 140 kg stationary cart. What is the final speed of the cart with the runner? Is kinetic energy conserved in this collision? Explain.

## Step-by-Step Solution

**Part 1: Final speed of the cart with the runner**

1. **Apply conservation of momentum:**
   Since there are no external horizontal forces, the total momentum of the system is conserved.
   Let $m_1 = 70 \text{ kg}$, $v_1 = 3 \text{ m/s}$, and $m_2 = 140 \text{ kg}$, $v_2 = 0 \text{ m/s}$.

2. **Set up the momentum equation:**
   $$ m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = (m_1 + m_2) v_f $$

3. **Solve for $v_f$:**
   $$ v_f = \frac{m_1 v_1}{m_1 + m_2} $$

4. **Substitute the values:**
   $$ v_f = \frac{70 \times 3}{70 + 140} = \frac{210}{210} = 1 \text{ m/s} $$

**Part 2: Is kinetic energy conserved?**

1. **Calculate initial kinetic energy ($K_i$):**
   $$ K_i = \frac{1}{2} m_1 v_1^2 = \frac{1}{2} (70) (3)^2 = 35 \times 9 = 315 \text{ J} $$

2. **Calculate final kinetic energy ($K_f$):**
   $$ K_f = \frac{1}{2} (m_1 + m_2) v_f^2 = \frac{1}{2} (210) (1)^2 = 105 \text{ J} $$

3. **Compare energies:**
   The final kinetic energy ($105 \text{ J}$) is less than the initial kinetic energy ($315 \text{ J}$).
   Therefore, **kinetic energy is not conserved**. This is an inelastic collision where the "lost" kinetic energy is transformed into other forms of energy (like heat or sound).
