# 2. String Harmonics

## Problem Statement
A guitar string is 64 cm long and has a fundamental frequency (one antinode) of 330 Hz. What is the speed of the wave on this string?

## Step-by-Step Solution

1. The fundamental frequency (first harmonic) of a string fixed at both ends corresponds to a standing wave with a single antinode.
2. Since the distance between consecutive nodes is half a wavelength ($\lambda/2$), the length of the string ($L$) forming one antinode is equal to exactly half of a wavelength:
   $$ L = \frac{\lambda}{2} $$
   Or alternatively:
   $$ \lambda = 2L $$

3. Convert the given length $L = 64\text{ cm}$ into meters:
   $$ L = 0.64\text{ m} $$

4. Calculate the wavelength:
   $$ \lambda = 2 \times 0.64\text{ m} = 1.28\text{ m} $$

5. The speed ($v$) of the wave on the string can be calculated from the fundamental frequency ($f = 330\text{ Hz}$) and the wavelength ($\lambda = 1.28\text{ m}$):
   $$ v = f \cdot \lambda $$

6. Substitute the values:
   $$ v = 330\text{ Hz} \times 1.28\text{ m} = 422.4\text{ m/s} $$
