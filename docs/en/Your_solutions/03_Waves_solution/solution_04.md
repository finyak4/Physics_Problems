# 4. Phase Difference

## Problem Statement
What is the phase difference in radians between two points on a wave that are separated by a distance of $\lambda/3$?

## Step-by-Step Solution

1. The relationship between the path separation ($\Delta x$) and phase difference ($\Delta \phi$) for a periodic wave shows that a full cycle ($2\pi$ radians) takes exactly one wavelength ($\lambda$).
   The proportion maps directly:
   $$ \frac{\Delta \phi}{2\pi} = \frac{\Delta x}{\lambda} $$
   Or equivalently:
   $$ \Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \cdot \Delta x $$

2. We are given the separation distance:
   $$ \Delta x = \frac{\lambda}{3} $$

3. Substitute this path difference into the formula:
   $$ \Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \times \left(\frac{\lambda}{3}\right) $$

4. The wavelength ($\lambda$) cancels out:
   $$ \Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{3} \text{ radians} $$
