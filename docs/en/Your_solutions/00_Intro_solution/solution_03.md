## 3. Proportionality

$F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$. Determine the factor by which $F$ changes if $r$ is doubled and both $m_1$ and $m_2$ are halved.

**Step-by-step solution:**

1. Let the new values be $m_1' = \frac{m_1}{2}$, $m_2' = \frac{m_2}{2}$, and $r' = 2r$.

2. Substitute the new values into the original equation to find the new force $F'$:

   $$
   F' = G \frac{m_1' m_2'}{(r')^2} = G \frac{\left(\frac{m_1}{2}\right) \left(\frac{m_2}{2}\right)}{(2r)^2}
   $$

3. Simplify the expression:

   $$
   F' = G \frac{\frac{1}{4}m_1 m_2}{4r^2} = \frac{1}{16} \left( G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2} \right) = \frac{1}{16}F
   $$

**Answer:** The force $F$ decreases by a factor of 16 (it is multiplied by $\frac{1}{16}$).
