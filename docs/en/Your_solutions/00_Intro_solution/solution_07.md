## 7. Logic & Series

A bicycle is 10 m from a wall, moving at $1\text{ m/s}$. A fly flies back and forth at $2\text{ m/s}$. Total distance?

**Step-by-step solution:**

1. Calculate the time it takes for the bicycle to reach the wall:

   $t = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}} = \frac{10\text{ m}}{1\text{ m/s}} = 10\text{ s}$

2. The fly constantly flies at $2\text{ m/s}$ during this exact same time period ($10\text{ s}$).

3. Calculate the total distance the fly travels:

   $d_{\text{fly}} = v_{\text{fly}} \cdot t = 2\text{ m/s} \cdot 10\text{ s} = 20\text{ m}$

**Answer:** The fly travels a total distance of $20\text{ m}$.
