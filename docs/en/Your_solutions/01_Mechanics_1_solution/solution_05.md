## 5. Relative Velocity

River velocity $\vec{v}_r = 2 \text{ m/s}$ East. Boat in still water $v_b = 5 \text{ m/s}$. The boat wants to go exactly North. River width $= 200 \text{ m}$.

**Step-by-step solution:**
1. Let North be the $y$-axis and East be the $x$-axis. River is $\vec{v}_r = (2, 0)$.

2. The boat's velocity relative to the water must have a westward component to exactly cancel the eastward river flow.

   $\vec{v}_{bw} = (v_{wx}, v_{wy}) \implies v_{wx} = -2 \text{ m/s}$.

3. We know the boat's speed in water is 5 m/s.

   $|\vec{v}_{bw}|^2 = (-2)^2 + v_{wy}^2 = 5^2 \implies 4 + v_{wy}^2 = 25 \implies v_{wy}^2 = 21 \implies v_{wy} = \sqrt{21} \approx 4.58 \text{ m/s}$.

4. **Direction:** The boat must head West of North by an angle $\theta$.

   $\sin\theta = \frac{2}{5} \implies \theta = \arcsin(0.4) \approx 23.58^\circ$ (West of North).

5. **Time to cross:** The northward velocity is $v_{wy} = \sqrt{21}$. The time to cross 200 m is:

   $t = \frac{\text{Width}}{v_{wy}} = \frac{200}{\sqrt{21}} \approx 43.64 \text{ s}$.

**Answer:**
- Heading: $23.58^\circ$ West of North (or pointing upstream against the river flow).
- Time to cross: $200/\sqrt{21} \approx 43.64 \text{ s}$.
