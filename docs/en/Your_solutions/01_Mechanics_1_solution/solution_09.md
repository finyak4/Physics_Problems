## 9. Momentum Comparison

Compare momentum of a 2-gram fly at $10 \text{ m/s}$ and a 60-gram tennis ball at $1 \text{ m/s}$.

**Step-by-step solution:**

1. Convert masses to standard SI units (kg).

   Fly: $m_f = 0.002 \text{ kg}$

   Ball: $m_b = 0.060 \text{ kg}$

2. Calculate momentum ($p = mv$) for each.

   Fly: $p_f = m_f v_f = 0.002 \text{ kg} \times 10 \text{ m/s} = 0.020 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$

   Ball: $p_b = m_b v_b = 0.060 \text{ kg} \times 1 \text{ m/s} = 0.060 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$

3. Compare the values.

   $0.060 > 0.020$, so the tennis ball has greater momentum.

**Answer:** The tennis ball has greater momentum ($0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s} > 0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$).
