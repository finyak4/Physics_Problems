## 8. Circular Motion

Centripetal acceleration on Earth's equator. $R = 6378 \text{ km} = 6.378 \times 10^6 \text{ m}$.

**Step-by-step solution:**
1. The formula for centripetal acceleration is $a_c = \frac{v^2}{R} = \omega^2 R$.


2. Find the angular velocity $\omega$ of the Earth:
   The Earth completes one rotation in approximately 24 hours (or more precisely, a sidereal day of $23.93$ hours, let's use $24 \text{ h}$ for simplicity).

   $T = 24 \times 3600 \text{ s} = 86400 \text{ s}$

   $\omega = \frac{2\pi}{T} = \frac{2\pi}{86400} \approx 7.272 \times 10^{-5} \text{ rad/s}$

3. Compute $a_c$:

   $a_c = (7.272 \times 10^{-5})^2 \times (6.378 \times 10^6)$
   $a_c \approx 5.288 \times 10^{-9} \times 6.378 \times 10^6 \approx 0.0337 \text{ m/s}^2$

**Answer:** The centripetal acceleration is approximately $0.0337 \text{ m/s}^2$.
