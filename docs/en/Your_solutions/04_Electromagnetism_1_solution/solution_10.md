# 10. Lorentz Force acting on Wire

**Problem:**
A straight wire $2.0\text{ m}$ long carries a current of $10\text{ A}$. It is placed in a uniform magnetic field of $B = 0.5\text{ T}$. Calculate the magnetic force on the wire if the angle between the wire and the magnetic field is:
a) $90^\circ$
b) $45^\circ$
c) $0^\circ$

**Solution:**
We are given:
* Length of the wire: $L = 2.0\text{ m}$
* Current: $I = 10\text{ A}$
* Magnetic field strength: $B = 0.5\text{ T}$

The magnitude of the magnetic force on a straight current-carrying wire is given by the formula:
$$ F = I L B \sin(\theta) $$
where $\theta$ is the angle between the direction of the current (the wire) and the magnetic field vector.

Let's compute the maximum possible force (when $\sin(\theta) = 1$):
$$ F_{max} = I L B = (10\text{ A})(2.0\text{ m})(0.5\text{ T}) = 10\text{ N} $$

Now we calculate the force for each given angle.

**a) Angle $\theta = 90^\circ$**
The wire is perpendicular to the magnetic field.
$$ F = F_{max} \sin(90^\circ) $$
$$ F = 10 \times 1 = 10\text{ N} $$

**b) Angle $\theta = 45^\circ$**
$$ F = F_{max} \sin(45^\circ) $$
$$ F = 10 \times \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} = 5\sqrt{2}\text{ N} $$
$$ F \approx 5 \times 1.414 \approx 7.07\text{ N} $$

**c) Angle $\theta = 0^\circ$**
The wire is parallel to the magnetic field.
$$ F = F_{max} \sin(0^\circ) $$
$$ F = 10 \times 0 = 0\text{ N} $$

**Answer:**
a) The force is **$10\text{ N}$**.
b) The force is approximately **$7.07\text{ N}$**.
c) The force is **$0\text{ N}$**.
