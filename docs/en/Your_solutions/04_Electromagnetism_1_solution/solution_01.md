# 1. Coulomb's Law

**Problem:**
Four point charges of +1.0 C each are placed at the corners of a square with sides of 1.0 m. Calculate the magnitude and direction of the electric force on a charge of -2.0 C placed at the center of the square.

**Solution:**
Let the four corners of the square be $A, B, C, D$ and the center be $O$. 
The charges at the corners are $q_A = q_B = q_C = q_D = +1.0\text{ C}$.
The charge at the center is $q_O = -2.0\text{ C}$.

According to Coulomb's Law, the electric force between two point charges is given by:
$$ \vec{F} = k \frac{q_1 q_2}{r^2} \hat{r} $$

The total force on the central charge $q_O$ is the vector sum of the forces exerted by each of the four corner charges:
$$ \vec{F}_{net} = \vec{F}_{AO} + \vec{F}_{BO} + \vec{F}_{CO} + \vec{F}_{DO} $$

Because the square is highly symmetric:
1. The distance $r$ from any corner to the center is the same for all four charges: $r = \frac{1.0\text{ m}}{\sqrt{2}} \approx 0.707\text{ m}$.
2. The magnitudes of the forces from the four identical corner charges on the central charge are all equal.
3. The force from the charge at corner $A$ ($\vec{F}_{AO}$) points directly opposite to the force from the charge at the diagonally opposite corner $C$ ($\vec{F}_{CO}$). Since they have the same magnitude and opposite directions, they perfectly cancel out: $\vec{F}_{AO} + \vec{F}_{CO} = 0$.
4. Similarly, the force from the charge at corner $B$ ($\vec{F}_{BO}$) perfectly cancels out the force from the charge at the diagonally opposite corner $D$ ($\vec{F}_{DO}$): $\vec{F}_{BO} + \vec{F}_{DO} = 0$.

Therefore, the net electric force acting on the charge at the center is exactly zero.

**Answer:**
The magnitude of the electric force is **$0\text{ N}$**, and it has no direction.
