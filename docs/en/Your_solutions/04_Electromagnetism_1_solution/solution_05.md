# 5. Field Levitation

**Problem:**
What electric field strength is required to make a proton levitate against Earth's gravity at the level of the Earth's surface? (Mass of proton $m_p \approx 1.67 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$, charge of proton $e \approx 1.6 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$, gravitational acceleration $g \approx 9.8\text{ m/s}^2$).

**Solution:**
For a proton to levitate, the upward electric force acting on it must exactly balance the downward gravitational force acting on it. 

The gravitational force (weight) is given by:
$$ F_g = m_p g $$

The electric force exerted by an electric field $E$ on a charge $q$ is:
$$ F_e = qE $$
In this case, the charge $q$ is the elementary charge $e$. So, $F_e = eE$.

Equating the two forces for equilibrium:
$$ F_e = F_g $$
$$ eE = m_p g $$

Solving for the electric field strength $E$:
$$ E = \frac{m_p g}{e} $$

Substitute the given values into the equation:
$$ E = \frac{(1.67 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg})(9.8\text{ m/s}^2)}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}} $$
$$ E = \frac{16.366 \times 10^{-27}}{1.6 \times 10^{-19}}\text{ V/m} $$
$$ E \approx 10.23 \times 10^{-8}\text{ V/m} $$
$$ E \approx 1.02 \times 10^{-7}\text{ V/m} $$

Since the proton is positively charged, the electric force must be directed upwards. Thus, the electric field must also be directed upwards (away from the center of the Earth).

**Answer:**
An electric field strength of approximately $\mathbf{1.02 \times 10^{-7}\text{ V/m}}$ directed upwards is required.
